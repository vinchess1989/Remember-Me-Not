Remember Me Not?

Bridging the Gap in Cognitive Care.
Startup Weekend Oulu, Finland • 17.04.2026 Team: Mary, Joel, Arnold, Eugenia, Vineeth

The Problem & The Gap

The Patient

Facing rapid, isolating memory degradation.

Experiencing high anxiety with abstract, clinical "brain games" that lack personal meaning.

The Guardian

Suffering from severe caregiver burnout.

Lacking daily progress visibility and bearing the massive emotional toll of being forgotten.

Existing Alternatives

Tools like Muistipuisto (Memory Park), offline activities, or Lumosity are disjointed.

They completely lack deeply personalized, family-linked daily ecosystems.

The €1 Billion Challenge

Finland spends roughly €1 Billion annually on direct dementia care. The financial bottleneck is severe.

85% Driven by 24-hour institutional care at advanced stages
€9.5k Average Annual Home Care Cost
>€28.4k Cost Triples when moving to Institutional Care

The Value Proposition

Delaying this transition through daily cognitive stimulation saves massive government funds while preserving family quality of life.

The Digital Solution

6 Core Patient Modules

Matching Game Puzzles My Images Therapeutic Music Contacts Daily Schedule

Familiar faces and personalized exercises rather than abstract tasks.

Guardian Empowerment

  • Intuitive dashboards to edit and monitor content.
  • Real-time tracking of identification accuracy and speed.

The Intelligence Layer

Powered by a spaced-repetition algorithm.

Example: If the patient starts forgetting "Mary", the app alerts the guardian to increase exposure to Mary's photos in the modules.

Business Model & Market Strategy

Customer Segments

  • B2G: Municipalities & Wellbeing counties (reducing budgets).
  • B2C: Guardians/Families (premium subscriptions).
  • Early Adopters: Early-stage patients struggling with communication.

Revenue Streams

  • B2G Licensing contracts.
  • B2C Premium Subscriptions.
  • Future Hardware Sales (Ecosystem expansion).

Key Metrics

  • Daily Active Usage (DAU).
  • Streak maintenance.
  • Identification accuracy over time.
  • Time delayed before institutional care transition.

Unfair Advantage: Hardware Moat

Phase 4 Strategy: Linking physical senses to digital memory recall.

Smart Olfactory Integration

App-linked Bluetooth scent diffusers to reinforce daily routines.

  • Emitting a coffee aroma at 8 AM to wake up gently.
  • Releasing lavender scent at 7 PM to reduce evening "sundowning" agitation.

Tangible Reminiscence Box

Physical objects equipped with NFC/RFID tags (e.g., a wedding veil, baby shoes, physical photos).

The Magic: Picking them up triggers the nearby tablet to automatically play a related video or audio note from a loved one.
